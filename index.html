Golf Academy — Master the Real Game

Welcome to the links! Golf is played over 18 holes on a course. The objective: complete each hole in as few strokes as possible. Your score is measured relative to par.

Scoring Terms

  • Ace / Hole-in-One: 1 stroke on any hole — the dream shot
  • Condor: 4 under par (-4) — virtually mythical
  • Albatross (Double Eagle): 3 under par (-3) — extremely rare
  • Eagle: 2 under par (-2) — outstanding
  • Birdie: 1 under par (-1) — great shot-making
  • Par: Expected strokes for the hole (3, 4, or 5)
  • Bogey: 1 over par (+1) — average for amateurs
  • Double Bogey: 2 over (+2) | Triple Bogey: 3 over (+3)
  • Snowman: Score of 8 on a hole — you want to avoid this

Course Anatomy

  • Tee Box: Starting area — you "tee off" from here
  • Fairway: The short-grass path from tee to green — keep it here
  • Rough: Longer grass flanking the fairway — harder to hit from
  • Green: Ultra-short grass around the hole — putt here
  • Bunker (Sand Trap): Sand-filled hazards — requires a special shot to escape
  • Water Hazard: Ponds, creeks, lakes — ball in = 1 stroke penalty
  • Fringe/Apron: The collar of grass between fairway and green
  • Dogleg: A hole that bends left or right — strategy matters
  • Pin/Flagstick: The flag marking the hole's location on the green

Shot Types & Swing Terms

  • Drive / Tee Shot: First shot, maximum distance off the tee
  • Approach Shot: Aiming for the green from the fairway
  • Chip: Short, low shot near the green — rolls more than it flies
  • Pitch: Higher, shorter shot that lands soft on the green
  • Putt: Rolling the ball along the green into the hole
  • Fade: Gentle left-to-right curve (right-handed) — controlled shape
  • Draw: Gentle right-to-left curve — adds distance
  • Slice: Uncontrolled fade — curves hard right (common beginner mistake)
  • Hook: Uncontrolled draw — curves hard left
  • Shank: Ball shoots sideways off the hosel — the most dreaded miss
  • Flop Shot: High, soft shot that stops quickly — Phil Mickelson's specialty
  • Punch/Stinger: Low, penetrating shot under wind — Tiger's go-to
  • Lay Up: Intentionally short shot to avoid a hazard

Common Golf Sayings

  • "Drive for show, putt for dough." — Putting wins tournaments
  • "Grip it and rip it." — Swing hard, worry later (John Daly style)
  • "It's not how, it's how many." — Ugly shots that go in still count
  • "Every shot makes someone happy." — Bad shots happen to everyone
  • "The ball doesn't know who's hitting it." — Trust the swing, not the nerves
  • "Play the course, not your opponent." — Focus on your own game
  • "A bad day of golf beats a good day at the office." — Enjoy the walk
  • "Swing easy, hit hard." — Tempo beats force every time
  • "Miss it on the correct side." — Course management is key
